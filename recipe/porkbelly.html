Pork Belly Rice

Description

Serves 2-4

A savory pork belly rice dish that uses Korean ingredients, simplified for an easy 15-minute weeknight dinner.

You can substitute any meat for the pork belly! Just make sure to adjust your cooking time accordingly for specific types of meat.

Ingredients

  • 2 green onions
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 1.3 lbs pork belly
  • 1/2 tbsp neutral-tasting cooking oil
  • 3-4 cups cooked rice, 1 cup/serving
  • soft boiled eggs, optional
  • toasted sesame seeds

Gochujang Samgyupsal Sauce

  • 2 tbsp Korean chili paste (gochujang)
  • 1 tbsp Korean chili pepper flakes (gochugaru)
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1/2 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1 tbsp light corn syrup
  • 2 tbsp mirin
  • black pepper, to taste
  • 1 tbsp water
  • 1 tsp cornstarch
  • MSG (optional)

Steps

Prep

  1. Thinly slice the green onions, separate whites and greens. Finely chop garlic.
  2. Cut the pork belly into bite-sized pieces.

Sauce Prep

In a small bowl, mix together all the ingredients listed in the sauce ingredients list. Mix well, and set aside.

Cooking

  1. In a large pan or wok, heat oil over high heat. Once pan is hot, add pork belly. Cook for 4-5 minutes or until golden brown and crispy.
  2. To the pan, add garlic and whites of green onions. Saute for 1 minute or until fragrant. Make sure not to burn the garlic.
  3. Reduce heat to medium-low. Pour in sauce, stir for 1-2 minutes until pork is completely coated. Add in the sesame oil, toss, and set aside.

Assembly

  1. Serve over rice, adding greens of green onions and sesame seeds to garnish.
